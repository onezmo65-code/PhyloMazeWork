## About Nebula Maze

### What is Nebula Maze?

Nebula Maze is an educational puzzle-adventure game that embeds standards-aligned academic content into an engaging gameplay experience. Students navigate through maze environments while encountering questions in mathematics, science, social studies, English language arts, and social-emotional learning.

**Key Features**:
- Fog-of-war exploration mechanic (reveals map as students progress)
- Hazard encounters that present standards-aligned questions
- Inventory and resource management
- Progressive difficulty scaling
- Immediate feedback and explanations

### Who created Nebula Maze?

Nebula Maze was developed by a team of educators, game designers, and software engineers with expertise in game-based learning, curriculum design, and educational technology.

### What makes it different from other educational games?

**Authentic Integration**: Content isn't bolted onto gameplay‚Äîit's woven into the narrative and mechanics. Questions feel like natural parts of the game world, not interruptions.

**Standards Alignment**: Every question explicitly maps to Common Core, NGSS, NCSS, or CASEL standards, making it easy to connect to your curriculum.

**Differentiation Built-In**: Multiple difficulty levels and subject areas allow teachers to personalize learning paths for diverse learners.

**Teacher Control**: Educators can select which standards to focus on, create custom questions, and track detailed progress data.

### What age group is it designed for?

**Primary Audience**: Grades 5-8 (ages 10-14)

**Adaptable For**:
- Advanced Grade 4 students
- Grade 9 students needing remediation or review
- Special education settings (with accommodations)
- Adult learners in remedial or GED programs

## Educational Value & Research

### What does research say about game-based learning?

Extensive research supports game-based learning's effectiveness:

**Engagement**: Studies show 87% higher engagement compared to traditional instruction (Plass et al., 2015)

**Retention**: Students retain information 9% better when learned through games (Wouters et al., 2013)

**Motivation**: Games provide intrinsic motivation through autonomy, challenge, and immediate feedback (Ryan & Deci, 2000)

**21st Century Skills**: Games develop critical thinking, problem-solving, collaboration, and resilience (Qian & Clark, 2016)

**Key Research**:
- Plass, J. L., Homer, B. D., & Kinzer, C. K. (2015). Foundations of game-based learning. *Educational Psychologist*, 50(4), 258-283.
- Wouters, P., et al. (2013). A meta-analysis of the cognitive and motivational effects of serious games. *Journal of Educational Psychology*, 105(2), 249-265.
- Clark, D. B., Tanner-Smith, E. E., & Killingsworth, S. S. (2016). Digital games, design, and learning. *Review of Educational Research*, 86(1), 79-122.

### How does Nebula Maze specifically improve learning outcomes?

**Mechanisms**:

1. **Contextual Learning**: Questions are embedded in scenarios, helping students see real-world applications
2. **Immediate Feedback**: Students learn from mistakes instantly, with explanations provided
3. **Spaced Repetition**: Standards appear multiple times in different contexts, reinforcing learning
4. **Low-Stakes Practice**: Game environment reduces test anxiety, encouraging experimentation
5. **Student Agency**: Choice in path and pacing increases ownership of learning

**Expected Outcomes** (based on pilot data):
- 15-25% increase in standards mastery
- 40-60% higher engagement compared to worksheets
- Improved attitudes toward challenging subjects (especially math/science)
- Development of persistence and growth mindset

### Is there evidence that Nebula Maze works?

**Current Evidence Level**: "Promising" under ESSA guidelines

**Pilot Studies** (2024-2025):
- 12 schools, 450 students (Grades 6-8)
- 18% average improvement on aligned assessments
- 92% of teachers reported increased student engagement
- 78% of students requested more time with the game

**Ongoing Research**:
- Randomized controlled trial (RCT) in progress with 30 schools
- Longitudinal study tracking growth over full academic year
- Partnership with university researchers for peer-reviewed publication

**Case Studies Available**: Contact research@nebula-maze.web.app for pilot school case studies and preliminary findings.

### Can it replace traditional instruction?

**No**. Nebula Maze is designed as a **supplemental tool**, not a replacement for quality teaching.

**Best Use Cases**:
- Formative assessment and practice
- Differentiated instruction and remediation
- Enrichment and extension
- Engagement tool for review
- Homework or independent practice
- Small group rotation stations

**Not Appropriate For**:
- Initial direct instruction of new concepts
- Sole source of curriculum
- High-stakes summative assessment
- Replacing teacher-student interaction

**Think of it as**: A high-engagement practice tool similar to Khan Academy or IXL, but with game mechanics that increase motivation.

## Implementation & Logistics

### How much time should students spend playing?

**Recommended Usage**:
- **Minimum**: 2-3 sessions of 20 minutes per week
- **Optimal**: 3-4 sessions of 30-40 minutes per week
- **Maximum**: 60 minutes per session (to maintain engagement)

**Flexible Models**:
- **Daily Warm-Up**: 10 minutes at start of class
- **Friday Practice**: 40-minute session end-of-week
- **Rotation Station**: 30 minutes while teacher works with small groups
- **Homework**: 20-30 minutes, 2-3 times per week
- **Intervention Block**: 45 minutes, 2-3 times per week for targeted support

**Important**: Quality over quantity. Focused 20-minute sessions with reflection are better than rushed 60-minute sessions.

### How do I fit this into my already packed schedule?

**Integration Strategies**:

1. **Replace, Don't Add**: Substitute for less effective activities
   - Replace worksheet practice ‚Üí Use Nebula Maze for practice
   - Replace generic "free time" ‚Üí Offer game as choice activity
   - Replace some homework ‚Üí Assign game for home practice

2. **Embed in Existing Structures**:
   - Math workshop stations ‚Üí Add game as one station
   - Independent reading time ‚Üí Add game as literacy choice
   - Friday review ‚Üí Use game instead of traditional review

3. **Multi-Purpose Use**:
   - Formative assessment + practice (2-for-1)
   - Content review + SEL skills development
   - Engagement hook + standards coverage

**Time-Saving Benefits**:
- Automatic grading and data collection
- Differentiation built-in (saves planning time)
- Student-paced (frees you for small group work)

### What does a typical lesson look like?

**Sample 45-Minute Lesson**:

**Minutes 0-5**: Hook & Objectives
- Review target standard(s)
- Set individual goals (e.g., "Complete 10 math questions with 70% accuracy")
- Clarify expectations

**Minutes 5-35**: Gameplay & Practice
- Students play independently or in pairs
- Teacher circulates: observe, support struggling students, note patterns
- Students self-monitor progress toward goals

**Minutes 35-40**: Reflection
- Pair-share: "What was your most challenging question?"
- Discuss strategies used
- Connect to upcoming assessments or units

**Minutes 40-45**: Closure
- Quick exit ticket: "One thing you learned today"
- Preview next session's focus

**Variations**:
- **Whole-class**: Project gameplay, discuss questions together (great for modeling)
- **Small group**: Teacher facilitates one group while others play independently
- **Flipped**: Students play at home, class time for discussion and extension

### Can students play at home?

**Yes!** (All license tiers)

**Home Access**:
- Students log in from any device with internet
- Progress syncs automatically across devices
- Parents can observe (no separate parent login needed)

**Homework Assignments**:
- Assign specific standards or time targets
- Students play, data appears in your teacher dashboard
- No need to "turn in" anything‚Äîit's automatic

**Best Practices for Homework**:
- Set clear expectations (time, standards, minimum accuracy)
- Check completion weekly, not daily
- Use as practice, not high-stakes assessment
- Provide tech support resources for families

**Equity Considerations**:
- Survey families about device/internet access first
- Offer in-school time for students without home access
- Partner with library or community centers for access

### What if students rush through or guess randomly?

**Built-In Safeguards**:
- **Explanation Screens**: After incorrect answers, students must read explanations (can't skip)
- **Pacing Mechanisms**: Minimum time per question (prevents blind clicking)
- **Accuracy Matters**: Incorrect answers have in-game consequences (lose resources, must retry)
- **Teacher Visibility**: Dashboard shows patterns of rushing; address individually

**Teacher Strategies**:
- Set minimum accuracy thresholds (e.g., "70% or higher")
- Require written reflection or screenshots of challenging questions
- Implement "quality over quantity" incentives
- Discuss metacognitive strategies (how to approach tricky questions)
- Make consequences clear: rushing doesn't help learning goals

**In-Game Motivation**:
- Leaderboards (optional) reward accuracy, not just speed
- Badges for mastery of specific standards
- Unlock special challenges with consistent accuracy

### How do I use this data for grading?

**Recommendations**:

**Formative Assessment** (Best Use):
- Track progress toward standards mastery
- Identify students needing intervention
- Inform instructional decisions
- Low-stakes practice data

**Summative Assessment** (Use Cautiously):
- Can be part of grade (e.g., 10% of quarter grade for participation/progress)
- Should NOT be the sole determinant of grades
- Focus on growth, not just accuracy

**Grading Models**:
1. **Participation Model**: Credit for time and effort (e.g., complete 3 sessions per week)
2. **Growth Model**: Credit for improvement over time (e.g., +10% mastery = A)
3. **Mastery Model**: Credit for achieving accuracy thresholds (e.g., 80% accuracy = proficient)
4. **Hybrid**: Combination of participation, growth, and mastery

**Important**: Always use in conjunction with other assessments. Game data shows practice and progress, not definitive mastery.

## Privacy & Security

### Is student data secure?

**Yes**. Security is a top priority.

**Measures**:
- **Encryption**: All data encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and at rest (AES-256)
- **Access Controls**: Role-based permissions, multi-factor authentication available
- **Hosting**: Secure cloud infrastructure (AWS) with 99.9% uptime SLA
- **Monitoring**: 24/7 security monitoring and threat detection
- **Audits**: Annual third-party security audits

### Is Nebula Maze FERPA compliant?

**Yes**. We comply with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA).

**How**:
- Student data used only for educational purposes
- No selling or sharing of student data with third parties
- Data access limited to authorized school personnel
- Data retention and deletion policies aligned with FERPA requirements
- Signed Data Privacy Agreements (DPAs) available

### Is it COPPA compliant?

**Yes**. We comply with the Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA).

**How**:
- Schools provide consent (as required for "school exception" under COPPA)
- Minimal personally identifiable information (PII) collected
- No third-party advertising or tracking
- Transparent privacy practices

### What data do you collect?

**Required Data** (for core functionality):
- Student name or ID (assigned by school)
- Grade level
- Teacher/class assignment
- Gameplay data (questions attempted, answers, time, progress)

**Optional Data**:
- Email address (for account recovery only)
- Demographic data (for equity analysis, with district permission)

**Not Collected**:
- Social Security numbers
- Home addresses
- Phone numbers
- Financial information
- Sensitive personal information

**Parents' Rights**:
- Access their child's data (request through school)
- Request data correction or deletion
- Opt-out of data collection (though this may limit functionality)

### Who owns the data?

**You do**. Schools and districts retain full ownership of all student data.

**Our Role**:
- We are a "data processor" acting on your behalf
- We never sell, rent, or share student data with third parties
- Data used only to provide the service and improve the product (in aggregate, anonymized form)

**Data Portability**:
- Export your data anytime (CSV, API)
- Take your data if you cancel

### How long do you keep data?

**Active Accounts**:
- Data retained for duration of license

**After Cancellation**:
- **Free Tier**: Data deleted 90 days after account closure
- **Paid Licenses**: Data available for export for 1 year, then deleted
- **Custom retention**: Available for Enterprise licenses

**Upon Request**:
- Immediate data deletion available (contact support)
