Frequently Asked Questions

How does Nebula Maze differ from other educational games?

Unlike many educational games that feel like "quizzes with graphics," Nebula Maze is a genuine game first. The fog-of-war exploration, survival mechanics, and inventory management mirror popular commercial games. Education is seamlessly woven in, not tacked on. Students play because it's fun, not because they have to.

What's the research backing game-based learning?

Extensive research shows that game-based learning increases engagement, improves retention, and promotes mastery learning. Studies by MIT, Stanford, and the Joan Ganz Cooney Center demonstrate that well-designed educational games can improve learning outcomes by 20-30% compared to traditional methods. Nebula Maze leverages proven mechanics: immediate feedback, scaffolded challenges, and intrinsic motivation.

How long does implementation take?

For individual teachers: Immediate. Just sign up and start playing. For school/district licenses: 1-2 weeks for account setup, teacher training, and content customization. We provide turnkey onboarding including teacher professional development, student orientation materials, and technical setup guides.

What training is required?

Minimal. If you can use Google Classroom or Canvas, you can use Nebula Maze. We provide: (1) 30-minute orientation video, (2) Written quick-start guide, (3) Optional 1-hour live training session, (4) Ongoing email support. Most teachers are fully comfortable within one class period.

Can I create my own content?

Yes! School and District licenses include a content creation tool. Upload questions, set difficulty levels, align to specific standards, and choose which units students encounter. Enterprise licenses include custom content development services.

What are the technical requirements?

Any device with a web browser: Chromebooks, iPads, laptops, desktops. Works on WiFi or cellular. Recommended: Chrome or Safari browser. No downloads or installations required. Works great even on older devices or limited bandwidth.
